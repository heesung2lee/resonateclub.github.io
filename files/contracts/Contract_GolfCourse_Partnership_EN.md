## 5. CANCELLATION AND REFUND POLICY

### 5.1 Cancellation by Agency (Standard)

| Notice Period (prior to scheduled tee time) | Cancellation Fee |
|---|---|
| **More than 14 days** | No charge |
| **7–14 days** | **[_____]%** of the total booking value (based on guaranteed headcount) |
| **72 hours – 7 days** | **[_____]%** of the total booking value (based on guaranteed headcount) |
| **Less than 72 hours** | **100%** of the total booking value (based on guaranteed headcount) |
| **No-show (no prior notice)** | **100%** of the total booking value (based on guaranteed headcount) |

### 5.2 Weather Cancellation

(a) **Course Closure:** If Course closes the golf course for play due to weather conditions (including but not limited to lightning, heavy rain rendering the course unplayable, or other hazardous conditions as determined by Course's golf operations management in its reasonable discretion), Agency shall receive a **full refund or credit** for all pre-paid amounts for that date's round. No cancellation fee shall apply.

(b) **Rain Check Policy:** If play has commenced and is suspended due to weather conditions, Course shall issue rain checks or pro-rated credits in accordance with Course's standard published rain check policy, which Course shall communicate to Agency in writing at the time of execution of this Agreement.

(c) **Agency Discretion Cancellation:** If the course remains open but Agency determines, in its reasonable discretion, that weather conditions are unsuitable for its clients' experience (e.g., sustained heavy rain), the standard cancellation schedule in Section 5.1 shall apply. Course shall, however, use good-faith efforts to reschedule the group to an alternative date within the same tour window at no additional fee.

### 5.3 Cancellation by Course

If Course must cancel a confirmed tee time booking due to course conditions, scheduled maintenance, or any other reason within Course's control, Course shall:

1. Provide written notice to Agency at the earliest possible time;
2. Offer a comparable alternative tee time on the same day or an adjacent day acceptable to Agency; and
3. If no acceptable alternative is available, provide a **full refund** of all pre-paid amounts.

If Course cancels due to a Force Majeure Event (Section 10), the provisions of Section 10 shall govern.

### 5.4 Refund Processing

All refunds due shall be processed by Course within **fifteen (15) business days** of the cancellation date.

| Coverage Type | Minimum Limit |
|---|---|
| Commercial General Liability | **$2,000,000** per occurrence / **$4,000,000** aggregate |
| Property Insurance | Full replacement value of Course property |

Course shall, upon Agency's request, provide a Certificate of Insurance naming **Resonate Club Inc. dba Resonate Tour** as an **additional insured** on the Commercial General Liability policy. Such certificate shall provide for at least **thirty (30) days'** written notice to Agency in the event of cancellation or material change.

### 6.2 Agency Insurance

Agency represents that it maintains general liability insurance and professional liability (errors & omissions) insurance appropriate to its tour operator business. Agency may, upon Course's reasonable request, provide evidence of such coverage.

### 6.3 Assumption of Risk / Waiver

Agency acknowledges that golf involves inherent risks, including but not limited to personal injury, property damage, and risks associated with outdoor activities. Agency shall ensure that each of its clients has executed a **Participant Waiver and Release of Liability** (in form and substance reasonably acceptable to Course) prior to the play date. Agency shall provide copies of all executed waivers to Course at least **seventy-two (72) hours** prior to the scheduled tee time or ensure that each client executes Course's standard on-site waiver at check-in.

### 6.4 Mutual Indemnification

(a) **Agency's Indemnity:** Agency shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Course, its affiliates, officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any and all third-party claims, losses, damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from or related to (i) Agency's breach of this Agreement; (ii) any negligent act or omission or willful misconduct of Agency, its employees, or agents; or (iii) any claim brought by an Agency client that is not directly caused by Course's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

(b) **Course's Indemnity:** Course shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Agency, its affiliates, officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any and all third-party claims, losses, damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from or related to (i) Course's breach of this Agreement; (ii) any negligent act or omission or willful misconduct of Course, its employees, or agents; or (iii) any personal injury or property damage occurring at Course's facility that is directly caused by Course's negligence or failure to maintain a safe premises.

### 6.5 Limitation of Liability

EXCEPT FOR A PARTY'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS UNDER SECTION 6.4, BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY, OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF PROFITS OR LOSS OF BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY THEREOF.

## 7. OPERATIONAL COMMITMENTS

### 7.1 Course Conditions and Presentation

Course shall present the course in a professional and well-maintained condition commensurate with its published standards and reputation. Course shall notify Agency as soon as reasonably practicable of any course conditions that may materially affect the playing experience (e.g., aerification, overseeding, significant construction, temporary greens).

### 7.2 Pace of Play

Course shall use reasonable efforts to maintain a pace of play of approximately **4 hours 30 minutes** or less per 18-hole round. Course shall enforce its standard pace-of-play policy and provide course marshals on duty during all group rounds.

### 7.3 Group Hospitality

Upon Agency's request, Course shall provide, at agreed additional cost:

- Dedicated check-in area or welcome signage for Agency's group
- Designated dining area for post-round meals
- Locker room access (if available)

## 9. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 9.1 Initial Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Agreement Date and continue for an initial term of **one (1) year** (the "**Initial Term**"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Section 9.

### 9.2 Renewal

This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive **one (1) year** renewal terms (each a "**Renewal Term**," and together with the Initial Term, the "**Term**"), unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least **sixty (60) days** prior to the end of the then-current Term.

### 9.3 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement for any reason or no reason upon **ninety (90) days'** prior written notice. Any confirmed bookings scheduled for dates falling within the notice period shall be honored on the terms of this Agreement unless both Parties agree otherwise in writing.

### 9.4 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

(a) Materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **fifteen (15) days** after receiving written notice identifying the breach in reasonable detail; or

(b) Becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, files a petition in bankruptcy, or has a petition in bankruptcy filed against it that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

### 9.5 Effect of Termination

Termination shall not affect any rights or obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including the obligation to pay for services rendered. Sections 4 (Payment Terms, to the extent of outstanding balances), 6 (Liability and Insurance), 8 (Confidentiality), 9.5 (Effect of Termination), and 11 (Governing Law) shall survive termination.

## 10. FORCE MAJEURE

Neither Party shall be liable for any delay or failure to perform its obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) to the extent such delay or failure is caused by circumstances beyond such Party's reasonable control, including but not limited to: acts of God; fire; flood; earthquake; hurricane or other severe weather; epidemic or pandemic (including related government restrictions); war; terrorism; civil unrest; labor strikes; governmental order, regulation, or action; or any other cause not within the reasonable control of the affected Party (each a "**Force Majeure Event**").

The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall (a) provide prompt written notice to the other Party describing the event and its expected duration, and (b) use diligent efforts to mitigate the impact and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable. If a Force Majeure Event prevents a confirmed booking from occurring, the Parties shall negotiate in good faith to reschedule, or, if rescheduling is not feasible within the Agency's tour window, all deposits and pre-payments shall be refunded in full.
