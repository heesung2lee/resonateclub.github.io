## 4. DRIVER REQUIREMENTS

### 4.1 Driver Qualifications

Provider warrants that every driver assigned to Agency's tours shall meet all of the following requirements at all times:

(a) Hold a valid **California Commercial Driver's License (CDL) Class B or higher** with a **Passenger (P) endorsement**;

(b) Maintain a current **Medical Examiner's Certificate** (DOT medical card);

(c) Have a clean driving record, defined as:

- No at-fault accidents within the preceding **three (3) years**
- No DUI/DWI convictions within the preceding **seven (7) years**
- No license suspensions or revocations within the preceding **five (5) years**
- No more than **one (1)** moving violation in the preceding **twelve (12) months**

(d) Pass a pre-service background check (criminal and driving record), which Provider shall conduct at its own expense;

(e) Be **fluent or conversant in English** (Korean language ability is a plus but not required); Agency may provide a Korean-speaking tour escort who will ride in the vehicle and provide interpretation;

(f) Be professionally attired (collared shirt, closed-toe shoes; uniform preferred) and present a clean, courteous, and service-oriented demeanor at all times;

(g) Maintain sobriety and be free from the influence of alcohol or any impairing substance while on duty.

### 4.2 Driver Conduct and Responsibilities

Each driver shall:

- Arrive at the scheduled pick-up location at least **fifteen (15) minutes** prior to the scheduled pick-up time
- Assist with loading and unloading of luggage and golf bags
- Follow the itinerary as provided by Agency unless modified by the Agency tour escort
- Maintain safe driving practices at all times, obeying all traffic laws
- Keep the vehicle clean throughout the service day
- Communicate proactively with Agency's tour escort regarding any delays, traffic conditions, or issues

### 4.3 Agency's Right to Reject

Agency reserves the right to request the removal and replacement of any driver who, in Agency's reasonable judgment, fails to meet the standards set forth above or whose conduct is unsatisfactory. Provider shall use best efforts to provide a suitable replacement within **four (4) hours** of receiving written notice.

## 5. VEHICLE BREAKDOWN AND BACKUP

### 5.1 Preventative Maintenance

Provider shall maintain all vehicles in accordance with manufacturer-recommended service intervals and in a safe, roadworthy condition. Provider shall perform pre-trip inspections prior to each day's service.

### 5.2 Breakdown Response

In the event of a mechanical breakdown, tire failure, or any condition rendering the assigned vehicle unsafe or inoperable during service ("**Breakdown**"), Provider shall:

(a) Immediately notify Agency's tour escort and provide an estimated resolution time;

(b) Dispatch a **replacement vehicle of equal or greater capacity and quality** to the group's location within:

- **Sixty (60) minutes** if the group is within the greater Los Angeles metropolitan area (defined as a 30-mile radius of LAX)
- **Ninety (90) minutes** if the group is outside the greater Los Angeles metropolitan area

(c) Ensure the replacement vehicle is clean, fueled, and ready to resume the itinerary without further disruption;

(d) Cover all costs associated with the replacement, including any towing of the disabled vehicle. Agency shall not be charged additional fees for the replacement.

### 5.3 Service Credit for Delays

If a Breakdown causes a delay exceeding **ninety (90) minutes** in the group's itinerary, Provider shall apply the following service credits:

| Delay Duration (cumulative) | Service Credit |
|---|---|
| 90 minutes – 3 hours | **25%** discount on that day's daily rate |
| 3 hours – 5 hours | **50%** discount on that day's daily rate |
| Over 5 hours | **100%** refund for that day's daily rate |

Service credits shall be deducted from the final invoice for the tour.

### 5.4 Backup Vehicle Availability

Provider represents that it has or has access to at least **one (1) backup vehicle** of comparable specifications that is available for dispatch during all scheduled tour dates. Provider shall disclose in advance any dates where backup vehicle availability may be limited.

2. Use best efforts to arrange a comparable alternative transportation provider at the same or lower rate, covering any reasonable cost differential;

If Agency cancels due to a Force Majeure Event, Section 11 (Force Majeure) shall govern. ## 8. LIABILITY AND INSURANCE

### 8.1 Provider Insurance — Minimum Requirements

Provider shall maintain, at its sole expense, the following minimum insurance coverage throughout the Term of this Agreement. **All policies shall be issued by insurers rated A- (A.M. Best) or better and licensed to do business in California.**

| Coverage Type | Minimum Limit |
|---|---|
| **Commercial Automobile Liability** (including owned, hired, and non-owned vehicles) | **$5,000,000** combined single limit (CSL) |
| Commercial General Liability | **$2,000,000** per occurrence / **$4,000,000** aggregate |
| Workers' Compensation | As required by California law |
| Employer's Liability | **$1,000,000** per accident |

### 8.2 Additional Insured and Certificate

(a) Provider shall name **Resonate Club Inc. dba Resonate Tour** as an **additional insured** on both the Commercial Automobile Liability and Commercial General Liability policies by endorsement.

(b) Provider shall deliver a **Certificate of Insurance** evidencing all required coverage and additional insured status to Agency no fewer than **five (5) business days** prior to the first service date and upon each policy renewal thereafter.

(c) The certificate shall provide that the insurer will endeavor to provide at least **thirty (30) days'** written notice to Agency prior to cancellation, non-renewal, or material reduction in coverage.

(d) Provider's insurance shall be **primary and non-contributory** with respect to any insurance maintained by Agency.

### 8.3 Passenger Safety and Liability

(a) Provider assumes full responsibility for the safe operation of all vehicles and the safety of all passengers while in transit.

(b) Provider shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws and regulations governing passenger transportation, including California Vehicle Code, California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC) regulations where applicable, and Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) regulations where applicable.

(c) Provider shall ensure all passengers are seated and wearing seat belts (where available) before the vehicle is in motion.

### 8.4 Indemnification

(a) **Provider's Indemnity:** Provider shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Agency, its affiliates, officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any and all third-party claims, losses, damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from or related to:

(i) Any personal injury (including death) or property damage arising from the ownership, maintenance, or operation of any vehicle used in performance of this Agreement;
(ii) Provider's breach of this Agreement;
(iii) Any negligent act or omission, recklessness, or willful misconduct of Provider, its employees, agents, or drivers;
(iv) Provider's failure to maintain the insurance coverage required under this Agreement.

(b) **Agency's Indemnity:** Agency shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Provider from third-party claims arising from Agency's breach of this Agreement or Agency's negligent acts, omissions, or willful misconduct.

### 8.5 No Limitation for Bodily Injury

Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Agreement, **nothing in this Agreement shall limit or exclude Provider's liability for personal injury (including death) or tangible property damage to the extent caused by Provider's negligence, recklessness, or willful misconduct.** The limitation of liability in Section 8.6 applies solely to indirect, consequential, and economic losses.

### 8.6 Limitation of Liability

EXCEPT FOR INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY, WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, AND THE CARVE-OUT IN SECTION 8.5, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF PROFITS.

## 10. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 10.1 Term

This Agreement commences on the Agreement Date and continues for an initial term of **one (1) year** ("**Initial Term**"), automatically renewing for successive **one (1) year** renewal terms, unless either Party provides **sixty (60) days'** written notice of non-renewal.

### 10.2 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate for any reason upon **ninety (90) days'** prior written notice. Confirmed bookings within the notice period shall be honored.

### 10.3 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

(a) Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within **fifteen (15) days** of written notice; or

(b) Becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, or files for bankruptcy (or has an involuntary petition filed that is not dismissed within 60 days).

### 10.4 Immediate Termination — Safety

Agency may terminate this Agreement immediately, without prior notice, if any vehicle used under this Agreement is involved in an accident resulting in serious injury or fatality while Provider is in material breach of the safety, insurance, or driver-qualification provisions of this Agreement.

### 10.5 Survival

Sections 7 (to the extent of outstanding cancellation fees), 8 (Liability and Insurance), 9 (Confidentiality), 10.5 (Survival), and 13 (Governing Law) shall survive termination.

---

## 11. FORCE MAJEURE

Neither Party shall be liable for failure to perform (other than payment obligations) due to circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including: acts of God; fire; flood; earthquake; severe weather; epidemic or pandemic (including government restrictions); war; terrorism; civil unrest; labor strikes not caused by the affected Party; or governmental order (each a "**Force Majeure Event**").

The affected Party shall provide prompt written notice and use diligent efforts to resume performance. If a Force Majeure Event prevents a confirmed booking, the Parties shall negotiate in good faith to reschedule. If rescheduling is not feasible, all deposits and pre-payments shall be refunded in full within **fifteen (15) business days**.
