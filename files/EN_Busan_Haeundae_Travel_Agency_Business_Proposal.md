## 2. Establishment Background & Vision

### 2.1 Founding Background

#### 2.1.1 Structural Growth of the Korean Golf Market

South Korea has established itself as one of the world's most golf-passionate nations.

* **Golf Population**: Approximately 6.24 million people (~11% of total population) play golf, representing an annual average growth of 8%+ over the past five years.
* **High-Net-Worth Golf Demand**: Post-pandemic, demand for overseas golf tours among high-income golfers (annual income KRW 100M+) has exploded. As of 2023, Korean outbound golf travelers are estimated at approximately 350,000 annually, with the United States being the second most preferred destination after Southeast Asia.
* **Premiumization Trend**: Korean golfers are shifting their consumption patterns from "value-for-money" golf travel to "value-for-heart" premium golf experiences.

#### 2.1.2 The Rise of US Inbound Tourism to Korea

* **K-Culture Boom**: The global spread of K-POP, K-Drama, K-Beauty, and K-Food has dramatically increased American travel demand to Korea.
* **US Visitors to Korea**: Approximately 1.1 million in 2023 (up 58% YoY), projected to surpass 1.3 million in 2024.
* **Luxury Niche Market**: An increasing number of high-income American travelers are moving beyond traditional group tours to seek private, curated Korean experiences.

#### 2.1.3 Founder's Competitive Advantage

* **20+ Years US Residency**: Founder Hee Sung Lee has resided in the United States for over 20 years, building an extensive network across California's premier golf courses, hotels, and transportation providers.
* **Industry Hands-On Experience**: Years of working directly at US golf courses have provided deep, practical understanding of golf industry systems and infrastructure — including tee time reservation systems, course management, and member services operations.
* **Bilingual & Bicultural Competency**: Fluent in Korean and English with deep understanding of both cultures, enabling precise identification of customer needs.
* **Digital-Native Approach**: Website-based online booking systems and digital marketing capabilities enable efficient one-person corporate operations.

### 2.2 Business Vision and Goals

#### Short-Term Vision (2026 - 2027)
Successfully enter the Korean outbound golf market and US inbound luxury travel market from a Busan Haeundae base, achieving **annual revenue of KRW 500M** and establishing brand recognition.

#### Mid-Term Vision (2027 - 2028)
**Surpass KRW 1 billion in annual revenue**, expand partner golf courses (California → Arizona, Florida), and grow the organization by hiring 2-3 dedicated staff.

#### Long-Term Vision (2028 - 2029)
Emerge as Asia's premier premium golf and travel platform. Expand beyond Korea↔USA routes (Korea↔Japan, Korea↔Vietnam) and grow to **KRW 2 billion in annual revenue**.

### 5.3 Establishment Procedure and Timeline

#### Stage 1: Corporation Establishment Preparation (Week 1)

* Finalize company name and business purpose
* Confirm registered office location and execute lease agreement
* Prepare Articles of Incorporation (notarized certification)
* Capital contribution (obtain stock payment custody certificate)
* Prepare incorporator (sole shareholder) meeting minutes

#### Stage 2: Corporation Registration (Weeks 1-2)

* Jurisdictional Registry: Busan District Court, Eastern Branch Registry Division
* Required Documents: Articles of Incorporation, Share Issuance Consent, Stock Payment Certificate, Letter of Acceptance of Office, Registration Application
* After registration completion, obtain Corporate Registration Certificate (processing time: ~3-5 business days)

#### Stage 3: Business Registration (Weeks 2-3)

* Jurisdictional Tax Office: Haeundae Tax Office
* Required Documents: Corporate Registration Certificate, Articles of Incorporation copy, Lease Agreement, Business Registration Application
* Processing time: ~5-7 business days

#### Stage 4: Domestic & International Travel Agency Registration (Weeks 3-6)

* **Competent Authority**: Busan Haeundae-gu Office (Culture & Tourism Division)
* **Required Documents**:
  * Tourism Business Registration Application (Enforcement Rules, Form No. 1)
  * Business Plan (based on this proposal)
  * Corporate Registration Certificate (including deletion entries)
  * Capital Contribution Verification (bank balance certificate)
  * Office Lease Agreement copy
  * Overseas Tour Escort Qualification Documentation (employment or engagement contract)
  * Facilities and Equipment Inventory
* **Processing Time**: Approximately 2-4 weeks after submission (20 days under Civil Petitions Act)

#### Stage 5: Business Surety Insurance (Weeks 6-7)

* **Surety Amount**: KRW 50,000,000 base (Tourism Promotion Act Enforcement Decree, Article 15; additional KRW 200M for planned tours)
* **Insurance Type**: Travel Agency Business Surety Insurance (Seoul Guarantee Insurance or specialized insurer)
* **Premium**: 0.5-1% of surety amount (estimated at KRW 500K-1M annually)
* Immediately subscribed upon receipt of Travel Agency Registration Certificate

#### Stage 6: Supporting Infrastructure Setup (Weeks 6-8, Parallel)

* Website launch (www.resonateclub.com, www.resonatetour.com)
* Booking and payment system implementation
* Accounting software and office equipment setup
* Overseas partner contract execution

| ⑦ Legal/regulatory changes | **Low** | **Medium** | 🟢 Low |
| ⑧ Single-person operation risk | **Medium** | **Medium** | 🟡 Moderate |

### 10.2 Detailed Risk Analysis and Mitigation

#### ① Initial Customer Acquisition Failure (🔴 Critical)

* **Risk Description**: Delayed initial customer acquisition as a new brand, causing revenue shortfalls and cash flow deterioration
* **Mitigation**:
  * Secure pre-launch bookings through personal networks and referrals (target: 3 bookings for first 6 months)
  * Aggressive discount promotion for first customers (30-50% discount) → Early-bird pricing
  * Complimentary influencer tours → leveraged for SNS viral marketing
  * Early inbound customer acquisition through US partner travel agencies
* **Monitoring**: Track monthly inquiries and conversion rates, quarterly strategy reassessment

#### ② Exchange Rate Fluctuations (🟡 Moderate)

* **Risk Description**: Won/Dollar exchange rate fluctuations eroding profitability (baseline assumption: 1 USD = KRW 1,300)
* **Mitigation**:
  * ±10% exchange rate fluctuation buffer incorporated into package pricing
  * Prepay major costs (airfare, golf courses) to lock in exchange rates
  * Leverage natural hedge effect from bidirectional business structure (inbound + outbound)
  * Quarterly exchange rate monitoring with price adjustments as needed
* **Acceptable Exchange Rate Range**: 1 USD = KRW 1,200 – 1,450 (break-even at KRW 1,100)

#### ③ Intensifying Large-Agency Competition (🟡 Moderate)

* **Risk Description**: Large agencies (HanaTour, ModeTour) aggressively entering the premium golf market
* **Mitigation**:
  * Differentiate on **quality and personalization**, not scale — hyper-personalized service large agencies cannot replicate
  * Pursue exclusive contracts for specific courses/regions (priority partnerships with 3 California golf courses)
  * Focus on customer relationships: "Host Model" where the CEO personally communicates with every customer
  * Maximize operational efficiency through accelerated digital transformation

#### ④ Natural Disaster / Pandemic (🟠 High)

* **Risk Description**: Complete suspension of international travel in the event of a global pandemic recurrence (e.g., COVID-19)
* **Mitigation**:
  * Clearly state fully refundable cancellation policy (builds trust)
  * Maintain pivot readiness to domestic golf/travel products
  * Permanent reserve liquidity of KRW 50M (emergency operating funds)
  * Mandate travel insurance to distribute mutual risk

#### ⑤ Single-Person Operation Risk (🟡 Moderate)

* **Risk Description**: Business interruption due to the sole representative's health issues or personal circumstances
* **Mitigation**:
  * SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) documentation with key partner companies
  * Maintain a constant pool of 2-3 reliable freelance guides
  * Secure corporate perpetuity (differentiator from sole proprietorship)
  * Build organizational structure through planned hiring of 2 full-time staff within 3 years
