## 2. Vision & Core Values

### 2.1 Founding Background

#### Structural Growth of the Korean Golf Market

- **Golf Population**: Approximately 6.24 million (~12% of population), growing at 8%+ CAGR
- **Overseas Golf Demand**: ~350,000 Korean outbound golf travelers annually; USA is #2 destination after Southeast Asia
- **Premiumization Trend**: Korean golfers shifting from "value-for-money" to "value-for-heart" premium golf experiences

#### The Rise of US Inbound Tourism to Korea

- **K-Culture Boom**: Global spread of K-POP, K-Drama, K-Beauty, and K-Food driving US travel demand
- **US Visitors to Korea**: ~1.1 million in 2023, projected to surpass 1.3 million in 2024
- **Luxury Niche**: Growing demand from high-income Americans seeking private, curated Korean experiences beyond traditional group tours

#### Founder's Competitive Advantage

- **20+ Years US Residency**: Extensive network across California's premier golf courses, hotels, and transportation providers
- **Golf Industry Hands-On Experience**: Years working directly at US golf courses — tee time systems, course management, member services
- **Bilingual & Bicultural**: Fluent in Korean and English with deep understanding of both cultures
- **Digital-Native**: Website-based booking systems and digital marketing capabilities for efficient one-person operations

## 10. Risk Analysis & Mitigation

### 10.1 Risk Assessment Matrix

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Rating | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| (1) Initial customer acquisition failure | High | Very High | ● Critical | Pre-secure bookings via personal network, early-bird discounts, influencer seed tours |
| (2) Currency fluctuations | Medium | Medium | ◐ Moderate | ±10% price buffer, advance payments to lock rates, natural hedge from bidirectional business |
| (3) Airfare spikes | Medium | Medium | ◐ Moderate | Partner airline group rates, alternative flight options |
| (4) Large competitor entry | Low | High | ◐ Moderate | Hyper-personalized service differentiation, exclusive course contracts |
| (5) Natural disaster / pandemic | Low | Very High | ◐ High | Full-refund cancellation policy, domestic product pivot capability, emergency liquidity reserve |
| (6) One-person operation risk | Medium | Medium | ◐ Moderate | SOP documentation, freelancer pool of 2-3 guides, 2 full-time hires within 3 years |

### 10.2 Crisis Response Protocol

## 12. Conclusion

### 12.1 Business Feasibility

#### Market
- 6.24M Korean golfers with outbound demand growing at 12.5% CAGR; clear premiumization trend
- 1.3M+ US visitors to Korea annually; K-culture-driven luxury inbound demand surging
- **Premium niche market** underserved by large travel agencies

#### Competitive Advantage
- Founder Hee Sung Lee's **20+ years US residency, golf course hands-on experience, local network, and bicultural fluency** — irreplaceable competitive edge
- **Digital-first one-person model** enables minimal fixed costs with high profitability
- Cost-plus 40% markup ensures **predictable revenue structure**

#### Financial
- Initial requirement of ~KRW 80M with annual fixed costs of ~KRW 13.6M — **industry-low operating costs**
- Conservative scenario (12 groups/year) still yields Year 1 operating profit of ~KRW 51.4M
- Additional growth engine upon comprehensive travel agency upgrade

### 12.2 Key Success Factors

1. **Niche Dominance**: Focus on hyper-personalized premium golf & travel — difficult for large agencies to replicate
2. **Founder Capability**: Unique combination of bilingual/bicultural network and digital marketing expertise
3. **Lean Operations**: One-person agility with minimal fixed costs for price and profit competitiveness
4. **Bidirectional Business**: Simultaneous inbound/outbound operations provide natural risk hedging
5. **Customer-Centric**: Founder-led "host model" builds trust and loyalty at every touchpoint

Through the **Resonate Tour** brand, we will set a new standard for premium Korea↔USA travel.

Resonate Tour is not just a travel agency — we are **"curators of moments where experience resonates,"** committed to advancing the premiumization and globalization of Korea's travel industry.
